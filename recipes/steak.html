Steak

Description

Steak is one of my favorite meats to cook. It is very versatile and can be cooked in various ways. My favorite method of cooking a steak is using the reverse sear method. Reverse searing is simply cooking the steak from the inside first to reach a target temperature to your liking then searing the outside og the steak on high heat to create a crust. Thus, making a perfectly cooked steak. Before I proceed to cook a steak, I must first make certain preparations. I personally like to dry brine my steak before cooking. I sprinkle a generous amount of kosher salt to the steak on all sides and leave it in the refridgerator uncovered for at least a day. Two days max. What this does is it soaks up moisture from the steak but also retains the flavor at the same time. You will have a much nicer sear this way.

Ingredients

  • Ribeye Steak
  • Kosher Salt
  • Course Black Pepper

Steps

  1. Remove steak from packaging and pat dry moisture with a paper towel
  2. Generously sprinkle all sides of your steak with kosher salt and place in your refrigerator, uncovered for at least one day. Two days max.
  3. Remove steak from refrigerator and let it rest until steak comes to room temperature
  4. Bake steak in the oven at 225 degrees Fahrenheit until the internal temperature of the steak reaches to 125 degrees or whatever your liking
  5. Sear all sides of the steak on an oiled pan on high heat for 1 minute each side
  6. Remove steak from heat and let it rest for 10 minutes before cutting and serving
